About Us

The CZU Fires started on August 16, 2020 as the result of a thunderstorm that produced close to 11,000 bolts of lightning and started hundreds of fires throughout California. As of Sep 5, 2020, 1,490 structures have been lost and 140 structures have been damaged by the massive fire. This is more than what was destroyed in the 1989 Loma Prieta earthquake 31 years ago in Santa Cruz.

Helen Klee, a member of the Pajaro Valley Quilt Association (PVQA) and the community since 1989, will organize this CZU Lightning Fire Quilt Project - A quilt drive for fire survivors in the CZU Lightning Complex Fire in Santa Cruz county. Our goal is to provide a quilt for every bed in each destroyed household to all fire survivors who have lost their homes.

This project is supported by the PVQA, and the Santa Cruz County Office of Emergency Services. Helen will coordinate with community volunteers, other California quilt organizations, local long-arm quilting services, fabric shops, local churches, and quilting supply manufacturers to seek assistance and donations in order to reach our goal.
